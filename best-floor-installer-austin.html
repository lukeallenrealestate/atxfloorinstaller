What to Look for in an Austin Floor Installer

Whether you choose us or another company, here is what you should demand from any flooring installer in Austin:

  1. Flooring-specific experience. Ask how many years they have been installing flooring specifically, not general contracting. Ask for references from recent projects similar to yours.
  2. Written, detailed estimates. Any installer who gives you a verbal quote or a single lump sum without breaking down materials, labor, and preparation costs is not being transparent.
  3. Proper licensing and insurance. Verify that the company carries general liability insurance and workers' compensation. If an uninsured worker is injured in your home, you could be liable.
  4. Moisture testing. Any installer working on a concrete slab in Austin should test for moisture before installation. If they do not mention it, ask. If they say it is not necessary, find someone else.
  5. Workmanship warranty. A written warranty on the installation work, separate from the manufacturer's product warranty. This protects you if the installation itself causes problems.
  6. Material acclimation. For hardwood and laminate, the installer should deliver materials to your home 5 to 7 days before installation to allow acclimation. If they plan to install the same day materials arrive, that is a red flag.

For a complete checklist, read our guide on questions to ask your flooring installer.
