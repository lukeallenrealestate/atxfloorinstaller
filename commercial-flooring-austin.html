Commercial Flooring Installation in Austin, TX

Your commercial space needs flooring that can handle heavy foot traffic, meet safety codes, and still look professional day after day. At ATX Floor Installer, we provide commercial flooring installation for businesses across Greater Austin, from downtown office towers to neighborhood restaurants and medical clinics. We understand that downtime costs you money, so we work efficiently and can schedule installations during off-hours to minimize disruption to your operations.

Whether you are building out a new lease space, renovating an aging storefront, or upgrading your office, we bring the same precision and reliability that has earned us the trust of Austin homeowners for over 15 years. Commercial projects demand specialized materials, proper subfloor preparation, and strict attention to building codes and ADA requirements, and we deliver on all of it.

Commercial Flooring Materials We Install

Commercial environments have different demands than residential spaces. The materials need higher durability ratings, easier maintenance, and often must meet specific safety and accessibility standards. Here are the primary commercial flooring options we install in Austin:

Luxury Vinyl Tile (LVT)

Commercial-grade LVT is the most popular choice for Austin offices, retail stores, and medical facilities. Unlike residential LVP, commercial LVT features a thicker wear layer (typically 20 mil or higher) that stands up to rolling chairs, heavy carts, and constant foot traffic. It is waterproof, easy to clean, and available in realistic wood and stone patterns that give your space an upscale look without the upscale price tag. LVT also provides excellent sound absorption, which matters in open-plan offices and busy waiting rooms.

Commercial Tile

Porcelain and ceramic tile remain the gold standard for restaurants, commercial kitchens, restrooms, and medical offices where sanitation and moisture resistance are critical. We install commercial-grade porcelain with slip-resistant finishes rated for wet environments and high-traffic zones. Large-format tiles are trending in Austin commercial spaces for their clean, modern look and fewer grout lines to maintain.

Polished Concrete Overlay

Many Austin businesses, especially those in converted warehouse spaces along East 6th Street and the East Austin corridor, are choosing polished concrete for its industrial aesthetic and nearly indestructible durability. We apply decorative concrete overlays and polish existing slabs to create a sleek, low-maintenance surface. Polished concrete is ideal for breweries, coffee shops, art galleries, and retail boutiques where a modern, minimalist vibe is the goal.

Rubber Flooring

Gyms, fitness studios, daycares, and healthcare facilities benefit from rubber flooring's shock absorption, slip resistance, and sound dampening. We install both rolled rubber and interlocking rubber tile systems. Rubber flooring is available in a range of colors and thicknesses to match your brand and performance requirements.

Industries We Serve in Austin

We have completed commercial flooring projects for a wide range of business types across the Austin metro area:

  • Office spaces — From coworking spaces in the Domain to corporate offices downtown, we install flooring that handles rolling chairs, heavy desks, and daily foot traffic. LVT and commercial carpet tile are the most common choices.
  • Restaurants and bars — South Congress, Rainey Street, East 6th, and the growing food scene across Austin demand flooring that resists spills, grease, and constant cleaning. We install commercial tile with proper drainage slopes and sealed grout.
  • Retail stores — First impressions matter. We help Austin retailers choose flooring that looks great under showroom lighting while standing up to shopping carts, foot traffic, and seasonal rushes.
  • Medical and dental offices — Healthcare environments require flooring that meets infection control standards, is easy to sanitize, and provides comfortable footing for staff on their feet all day. Sheet vinyl and commercial LVT are popular choices.
  • Gyms and fitness studios — Austin's fitness culture demands flooring that absorbs impact, resists moisture, and holds up to dropped weights. We install rubber flooring systems designed for these environments.

ADA Compliance and Safety Standards

Commercial flooring must meet the Americans with Disabilities Act requirements for accessibility. This includes proper transition strips between different flooring surfaces, slip-resistance ratings that meet or exceed ASTM standards, and smooth, level surfaces that accommodate wheelchairs and mobility devices. Our installation team understands these requirements and ensures every project meets current ADA guidelines. We also follow all local Austin building codes and obtain any necessary permits for your project.

Slip resistance is especially important in Austin's commercial spaces. We select materials with appropriate Coefficient of Friction (COF) ratings for each application. Restaurants and commercial kitchens require higher COF ratings than office lobbies, and we specify materials accordingly to protect your customers and employees.

Durability and Maintenance Considerations

Commercial flooring is an investment, and we help you choose materials that balance upfront cost with long-term durability and maintenance requirements. Key factors we discuss with every commercial client include:

  • Wear layer thickness — For LVT, we recommend a minimum 20-mil wear layer for moderate commercial use and 28 mil or higher for heavy traffic areas.
  • PEI rating for tile — We specify PEI Class IV or V porcelain tile for commercial applications, ensuring it can handle the abrasion of daily commercial use.
  • Maintenance schedules — We provide detailed care instructions for every installation so your cleaning staff knows exactly how to maintain your new floors and protect your investment.
  • Warranty coverage — We work with manufacturers that offer commercial warranties, typically ranging from 10 to 20 years depending on the product.

Our Commercial Installation Process

Commercial projects require more planning and coordination than residential work. Here is how we approach every commercial flooring project:

  1. Site assessment and consultation. We visit your space, take measurements, evaluate the existing subfloor, and discuss your timeline, budget, and design preferences.
  2. Material specification and proposal. We provide a detailed proposal with product specifications, total cost, and a projected installation timeline.
  3. Scheduling and coordination. We work around your business hours. Evenings, weekends, and phased installations are all options to keep your business running.
  4. Subfloor preparation. Commercial subfloors often need leveling, moisture mitigation, or repairs before new flooring goes down. We handle all prep work in-house.
  5. Installation and final walkthrough. Our crew installs your flooring to manufacturer specifications, cleans the site thoroughly, and walks through the finished project with you before we consider the job complete.
