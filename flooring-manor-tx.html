Flooring Installation for Manor's Master-Planned Communities

Manor has emerged as one of the most exciting growth stories east of Austin. Located just fifteen minutes from downtown along US-290, Manor offers homebuyers something increasingly rare in the Austin metro: genuinely affordable new construction in well-designed master-planned communities. Developments like ShadowGlen, Presidential Meadows, Stonewater, Bell Farms, and Manor Crossing have attracted thousands of families who want modern homes, good schools in Manor ISD, and a short commute to Austin's tech and healthcare employment centers. ATX Floor Installer serves Manor homeowners with professional flooring installation that elevates these homes from builder-standard to truly personalized living spaces.

The housing stock in Manor is predominantly new construction from the past decade, built by production builders who prioritize efficiency and value. While these homes offer excellent floor plans and solid construction, the flooring packages tend to be the first area where builders cut costs. Thin carpet in bedrooms, basic vinyl in wet areas, and entry-level tile in kitchens are common across Manor's neighborhoods. Upgrading these materials is the single most impactful home improvement a Manor homeowner can make, transforming the look and feel of every room while building equity in a market where home values continue to climb.

Understanding Manor's Housing and Flooring Needs

Manor's homes present specific flooring considerations that our team understands well. Nearly all construction sits on concrete slab foundations, which influences material selection and installation methods. The black clay soils east of Austin are highly expansive, meaning they swell when wet and shrink when dry, creating subtle foundation movement that flooring must accommodate. We select products and installation techniques that work with these conditions rather than against them, ensuring your new floors maintain their appearance and integrity through Central Texas weather cycles.

The open-concept floor plans that dominate Manor's newer developments create both opportunities and challenges for flooring installation. A continuous run of hardwood or LVP flowing from the front door through the living room, kitchen, and dining area creates a stunning visual impact. But it also requires careful planning around transitions, undercutting door frames, and managing expansion gaps over large continuous areas. Our experience in Manor homes means we handle these details expertly, delivering seamless results that look intentional and refined.

Hardwood Flooring Installation

Hardwood flooring brings a level of warmth and sophistication to Manor homes that immediately sets them apart from neighboring properties with builder-standard surfaces. Engineered hardwood is our primary recommendation for Manor's slab-on-grade homes, offering the authentic beauty of real wood with the dimensional stability needed for concrete foundations and East Texas humidity. White oak remains the most popular choice among Manor homeowners for its clean, versatile aesthetic that pairs well with both modern and traditional decor. We also install hickory for families who want a harder wood species that better resists the dents and scratches of active household life. Our installation includes thorough slab moisture testing, vapor barrier installation, and precision fitting that accounts for the large, open rooms typical of ShadowGlen and Presidential Meadows floor plans.

Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP)

LVP is the most popular flooring choice in Manor by a significant margin, and it is perfectly suited to this market. Manor homeowners are value-conscious buyers who want attractive, durable flooring that performs well across every room without stretching the budget. LVP delivers exactly that. Modern rigid-core LVP products feature realistic wood-grain textures and colors that are virtually indistinguishable from real hardwood at a glance, with the added benefits of being completely waterproof, scratch-resistant, and easy to maintain. For families in Stonewater and Bell Farms replacing builder carpet throughout their home, LVP provides a dramatic visual upgrade at a fraction of the cost of hardwood. We install LVP as a floating floor over Manor's concrete slabs, with proper underlayment for sound dampening and moisture protection. The click-lock system allows for efficient installation with minimal disruption to your household, and most whole-home LVP projects in Manor are completed within two to three days.

Tile Installation

Tile is a practical and attractive choice for Manor kitchens, bathrooms, laundry rooms, and entryways. Many Manor homes came with basic ceramic tile in a single neutral color that serves its purpose but lacks personality. Upgrading to large-format porcelain, textured stone-look tile, or wood-look planks gives these high-use areas a completely different character. We install tile in all standard and custom patterns, from clean grid layouts in kitchens to herringbone and chevron designs in bathroom floors. For Manor homeowners undertaking bathroom renovations, we handle complete shower and tub surround installations including waterproof membrane systems, niches, and linear drains. In kitchens, we install durable porcelain floor tile that coordinates with countertops and cabinetry for a cohesive, polished look. Our installers take particular care with the grout work and edge finishing that separates professional tile installation from the shortcuts sometimes found in production-built homes.

Hardwood Refinishing

While Manor is primarily a new-construction market, the earliest neighborhoods in the area are now old enough that their hardwood floors are showing wear. Homes built in the ShadowGlen golf course community during the mid-2000s and early 2010s often feature hardwood in main living areas that has accumulated over a decade of daily use. Scratched surfaces, worn finish in high-traffic paths between kitchen and living room, and outdated stain colors are common concerns. Our refinishing service addresses all of these issues by sanding the floors down to bare wood, repairing any damaged sections, applying a fresh stain in your chosen color, and sealing with multiple coats of commercial-grade polyurethane. The result is a floor that looks newly installed at roughly half the cost of replacement. We also refinish hardwood in older Manor properties closer to the original town center that have character-rich floors worth preserving rather than replacing.
