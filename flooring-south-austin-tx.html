Flooring That Fits South Austin's Unique Style

South Austin is unlike anywhere else in the city. The 78704 and 78745 zip codes encompass a vibrant mix of architectural styles, from the cozy 1950s bungalows near Zilker Park to the modern new builds lining South Lamar Boulevard. This is the part of Austin where creativity, individuality, and a laid-back lifestyle converge, and the flooring in these homes should reflect that energy. ATX Floor Installer has been helping South Austin homeowners find the perfect flooring match for years.

Whether you are renovating a craftsman cottage in Barton Hills, updating a ranch-style home off Manchaca Road, or finishing a condo conversion along South Congress, our team delivers professional results with the personal attention that South Austin residents value. We understand that budgets vary widely in this diverse community, and we work with every homeowner to find the right materials and installation approach for their situation.

Understanding South Austin Housing Stock

The homes in South Austin span nearly every decade and building style. Near Zilker, you will find compact mid-century homes with original hardwood or terrazzo floors. In Barton Hills, split-level designs with a mix of carpet and tile are common. Along the South Lamar corridor, newer townhomes and condos feature concrete subfloors that require specific installation methods. Further south in the 78745 zip code, ranch-style homes from the 1970s and 1980s often have outdated vinyl or carpet that is overdue for replacement.

This variety means we need to be versatile. Our installers assess each property individually, accounting for subfloor type, moisture conditions, existing floor removal needs, and the homeowner's design preferences. We never apply a one-size-fits-all approach in South Austin because no two homes here are alike.

Hardwood Flooring Installation

Hardwood flooring brings warmth and character to South Austin's eclectic homes. In Zilker and Barton Hills, where homes often feature open living areas that flow into dining spaces and kitchens, a continuous hardwood floor creates visual unity and expands the sense of space. We install oak, maple, and hickory hardwood in both nail-down and glue-down applications depending on your subfloor. For South Austin homeowners who love a natural, organic aesthetic, we offer matte finishes and wire-brushed textures that complement the neighborhood's casual vibe.

Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP)

LVP is one of the most popular flooring choices in South Austin, and for good reason. Many homes in this area have active households with pets, children, and a steady flow of foot traffic from Barton Springs outings and backyard barbecues. LVP handles all of it with ease while looking great. It is also one of the most budget-friendly ways to transform a space, which appeals to South Austin homeowners who want impact without breaking the bank. We install LVP over concrete slabs, plywood subfloors, and even over existing tile in some cases.

Learn more about LVP flooring →

Tile Installation

Tile is a smart choice for South Austin's warm climate. Porcelain and ceramic tile keep interiors cool during the long Texas summers and are exceptionally easy to maintain. In South Austin, we see strong demand for tile in kitchens, bathrooms, laundry rooms, and sunrooms. Patterned cement tile has also become popular in South Austin's design-forward homes, adding personality to entryways and kitchen floors. Our installers handle everything from simple grid layouts to complex geometric patterns with precision.

Learn more about tile installation →

Hardwood Refinishing

Many of the older homes in South Austin have original hardwood floors that have been hidden under carpet for decades. Pulling back that carpet often reveals red oak or pine floors with incredible potential. Our refinishing service breathes new life into these forgotten floors through professional sanding, staining, and sealing. For South Austin homeowners who appreciate the history and character of their homes, refinishing original hardwood is a way to honor the past while creating a fresh, modern look.
