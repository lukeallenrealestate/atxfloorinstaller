Expert Flooring for West Austin's Character Homes

West Austin is a neighborhood defined by its tree-lined streets, eclectic architecture, and deeply rooted sense of community. From the charming mid-century bungalows of Tarrytown to the custom estates perched above the Colorado River in Rollingwood, this part of the city blends old Austin charm with modern sophistication. ATX Floor Installer specializes in helping West Austin homeowners update, restore, and transform their floors to match the character and quality of these beloved homes.

Many homes in West Austin were built in the 1940s through 1970s and still feature original hardwood floors, dated tile, or worn-out carpeting. These properties present unique opportunities for flooring upgrades that respect the original architecture while introducing contemporary style and durability. Our team has extensive experience working in older homes where subfloor conditions, uneven surfaces, and historical considerations require a more thoughtful approach.

Flooring Challenges in Older West Austin Homes

Renovating flooring in an established neighborhood like Tarrytown or Lost Creek comes with challenges that newer construction does not. Pier-and-beam foundations common in older West Austin homes can create uneven subfloors that require leveling before new flooring can be installed. Original hardwood floors may have been covered by carpet decades ago and could be salvageable with professional refinishing. Our team evaluates each project individually, providing honest assessments and tailored solutions.

We also understand the importance of working respectfully in established neighborhoods. Our crews keep job sites clean, minimize noise disruption, and protect landscaping and finished surfaces throughout the installation process. Many of our West Austin clients are updating their homes while living in them, so we coordinate schedules to minimize inconvenience.

Hardwood Flooring Installation

Hardwood floors are a natural fit for West Austin's classic homes. Whether you are restoring original red oak floors in a Tarrytown cottage or installing new wide-plank white oak in a Rollingwood remodel, our team delivers results that honor the home's character. We offer solid hardwood for traditional installations and engineered hardwood for slab foundations or areas where moisture concerns exist. Popular choices among West Austin homeowners include white oak with a natural matte finish, hickory for a more rustic aesthetic, and walnut for a dramatic contemporary look.

Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP)

LVP has become a go-to flooring option for West Austin homeowners looking for the warmth and appearance of hardwood combined with waterproof performance. In homes near Lake Austin and the creek beds that wind through Lost Creek and Cat Mountain, moisture resistance is a practical necessity. Modern LVP products replicate the grain patterns and texture of real wood so convincingly that many visitors cannot tell the difference. We install premium brands that offer superior wear layers and realistic visuals.

Learn more about LVP flooring →

Tile Installation

Tile is a staple in West Austin homes, particularly in kitchens, bathrooms, and entryways. Many older homes feature original tile that has become outdated or damaged. We remove old tile and install modern porcelain, ceramic, or natural stone in its place. From subway tile backsplashes in renovated Tarrytown kitchens to large-format porcelain in Rollingwood bathroom remodels, our tile work is precise, level, and built to last. We also handle complex installations including shower surrounds, heated floor systems, and custom mosaics.

Learn more about tile installation →

Hardwood Refinishing

Some of the best hardwood floors in Austin are hiding under carpet in West Austin homes. We regularly pull back old carpet in Tarrytown and Bryker Woods homes to discover original red oak or pine floors that simply need professional refinishing. Our dustless sanding process minimizes mess, and our staining options range from natural clear coats to trendy grays and rich espresso tones. Refinishing your existing hardwood is one of the most cost-effective ways to dramatically improve your home's interior.
