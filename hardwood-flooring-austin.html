Solid Hardwood vs. Engineered Hardwood

Choosing between solid and engineered hardwood is one of the first decisions you will make. Both are excellent options, and the right choice depends on your home, your lifestyle, and where the floors will be installed.

Solid Hardwood

Solid hardwood planks are milled from a single piece of timber, typically three-quarters of an inch thick. They can be sanded and refinished multiple times over their lifespan, often lasting 50 years or more. Solid hardwood is ideal for main living areas, bedrooms, and dining rooms on above-grade floors. Because it expands and contracts with humidity, we do not recommend solid hardwood for basements or below-grade installations.

Engineered Hardwood

Engineered hardwood features a real hardwood veneer bonded to multiple layers of plywood or high-density fiberboard. This cross-layer construction makes it more dimensionally stable, which is a real advantage in the Austin climate where humidity can swing throughout the year. Engineered hardwood can be installed on any level of the home, including over concrete slabs and radiant heating systems.

Popular Hardwood Species We Install

We work with a wide range of domestic and exotic wood species. Here are the most popular choices among our Austin customers:

  • White Oak — The most popular choice in Austin right now. Durable, takes stain beautifully, and has a timeless grain pattern. Rated 1,360 on the Janka hardness scale.
  • Red Oak — A classic with warm, pinkish undertones and a pronounced grain. Slightly softer than white oak but still very durable for residential use.
  • Maple — A light, clean-looking hardwood with a subtle grain. Harder than oak (Janka 1,450), making it a strong choice for high-traffic areas.
  • Walnut — Rich, dark chocolate tones with a luxurious feel. Softer than oak, so best suited for bedrooms, studies, and low-traffic spaces.
  • Hickory — One of the hardest domestic species (Janka 1,820). Dramatic grain variation gives it a rustic, character-rich appearance.

Not sure which species is right for your home? We bring samples to your in-home consultation so you can see how each option looks in your space and lighting.

Our Hardwood Installation Process

Every hardwood installation follows a proven sequence that ensures beautiful, long-lasting results:

  1. In-home consultation and measurement. We visit your home, measure each room, discuss your preferences, and provide a detailed written estimate at no cost.
  2. Material selection and ordering. Once you choose your species, width, and finish, we order from trusted suppliers and schedule your install date.
  3. Acclimation. Hardwood needs to adjust to your home's temperature and humidity before installation. We deliver material 5 to 7 days early and store it on-site in the rooms where it will be installed.
  4. Subfloor preparation. We remove existing flooring, inspect and repair the subfloor, check for moisture issues, and ensure the surface is level. This step is critical to avoiding squeaks, gaps, and premature wear.
  5. Installation. Depending on the product and your subfloor, we use nail-down, glue-down, or floating installation methods. Our installers are meticulous about staggering joints, maintaining expansion gaps, and achieving tight seams.
  6. Finishing and cleanup. We install transitions, baseboards, and trim. We vacuum thoroughly, do a final inspection with you, and make sure you are completely satisfied.

Subfloor Preparation Matters

The quality of your hardwood installation depends heavily on what is underneath. A subfloor that is uneven, damp, or structurally compromised will cause problems no matter how good the hardwood is on top. Our team checks for moisture content, flatness, and structural integrity before any plank goes down. If repairs are needed, we handle them as part of the project so there are no surprises.

Hardwood Flooring Costs in Austin

Hardwood flooring costs vary based on the species, plank width, and complexity of the installation. Here are typical price ranges for our Austin projects:

  • Solid hardwood (material + labor): $8 to $15 per square foot
  • Engineered hardwood (material + labor): $7 to $13 per square foot
  • Subfloor repair (if needed): $1 to $3 per square foot
  • Removal of existing flooring: $1 to $2 per square foot

Every home is different, and the best way to get an accurate price is to schedule a free in-home estimate. We provide a detailed, line-item quote with no hidden fees.
