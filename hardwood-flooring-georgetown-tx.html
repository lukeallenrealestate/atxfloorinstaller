Hardwood Floor Installation in Georgetown, TX

Georgetown combines small-town Texas charm with one of the fastest-growing housing markets in the Austin metro. From the Victorian-era homes lining the historic downtown square to the active adult community of Sun City and the newer master-planned neighborhoods in Wolf Ranch and Georgetown Village, this city has a remarkable range of homes that benefit from professional hardwood flooring. ATX Floor Installer serves Georgetown homeowners with expert hardwood installation that respects the character of each property while delivering modern performance and beauty.

Georgetown's Unique Hardwood Flooring Needs

Sun City Active Adult Community

Sun City is the largest active adult community in Central Texas, with over 9,500 homes. Many residents moved in during the early to mid-2000s and are now ready for flooring upgrades. The original builder flooring, typically carpet and basic tile, has reached the end of its useful life after 15 to 20 years of use. Sun City homeowners are upgrading to engineered hardwood for its timeless beauty and the practical benefit of easier cleaning and maintenance compared to carpet. White oak in a natural or light stain is the dominant choice, creating bright, airy interiors that complement the open floor plans common in Sun City homes.

For Sun City residents specifically, we emphasize low-maintenance finishes and smooth transitions between rooms to reduce trip hazards. Many Sun City hardwood installations include coordinated transitions into tile bathrooms and direct entries from the garage.

Berry Creek and Cimarron Hills

Berry Creek is one of Georgetown's premier golf course communities, with homes ranging from the 1990s to present day. Many older Berry Creek homes have original hardwood floors that have dulled, scratched, or developed gaps over the years. For these properties, we offer both hardwood refinishing to restore existing floors and new hardwood installation to extend hardwood into rooms that currently have carpet or tile.

Cimarron Hills is Georgetown's luxury enclave, featuring estate-sized homes on large lots with hill country views. Hardwood in these homes tends toward wider planks (7 inches or more), premium European oak, and custom stain colors that complement the high-end finishes throughout. We work with architects and designers on Cimarron Hills projects to ensure the flooring integrates perfectly with the overall design vision.

Wolf Ranch and Georgetown Village

These newer communities represent Georgetown's growth corridor along I-35 and Williams Drive. Homes here were built between 2015 and the present, often with builder-grade LVP, laminate, or carpet that homeowners want to upgrade. Engineered hardwood is the most popular upgrade in these neighborhoods, adding warmth and value that the original flooring cannot match. The open floor plans in Wolf Ranch homes are particularly well-suited for continuous hardwood installation that flows from the entry through the living room, kitchen, and dining areas.

Popular Species for Georgetown Homes

  • White Oak — Georgetown's top choice. Works equally well in Sun City's casual interiors and Cimarron Hills' luxury finishes. Takes stain beautifully from natural to dark walnut.
  • Hickory — The rustic grain pairs perfectly with Georgetown's hill country aesthetic. Extremely hard (Janka 1,820) for maximum durability.
  • Walnut — Rich dark tones for formal dining rooms and studies in Berry Creek and Cimarron Hills. Softer than oak, best for low-traffic areas.
  • Pecan — A Texas native species with warm tones and moderate hardness. Increasingly popular among Georgetown homeowners who want a local, distinctive look.
