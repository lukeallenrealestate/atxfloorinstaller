Hardwood Floor Installation in Round Rock, TX

Round Rock is one of the fastest-growing cities in Texas, and its homeowners are investing in flooring that matches the quality of life this community offers. Why Round Rock Homeowners Choose Hardwood

Hardwood flooring transforms the look and feel of a Round Rock home in ways that no other flooring material can match. The warmth of real wood grain, the solid feel underfoot, and the undeniable curb appeal make hardwood one of the most requested upgrades we see in the Round Rock market. For families in neighborhoods like Cat Hollow and Behrens Ranch, hardwood adds both daily enjoyment and long-term value to homes that were often delivered with basic carpet and tile.

Round Rock's real estate market is competitive, and homes with hardwood floors consistently command higher prices and sell faster than comparable properties with carpet or laminate. According to local real estate data, hardwood flooring can add 3 to 5 percent to a home's resale value, making it one of the best returns on investment for any home improvement project.

Engineered Hardwood for Round Rock's Slab Foundations

Nearly every home in Round Rock sits on a concrete slab foundation. This is an important consideration when choosing hardwood, because solid hardwood should generally not be installed directly over concrete. The reason is moisture: concrete slabs allow moisture vapor from the soil to migrate upward, and solid hardwood absorbs that moisture, leading to warping, cupping, and buckling over time.

Engineered hardwood is the ideal solution for Round Rock homes. It features a real hardwood veneer on top, bonded to multiple layers of plywood or high-density fiberboard. This cross-layered construction makes it dimensionally stable against the humidity swings that are common in Central Texas, where summer moisture levels can climb above 70 percent and winter conditions drop well below 40 percent. Engineered hardwood can be glued directly to the slab or installed as a floating floor over a moisture barrier underlayment.

For more on the differences between engineered and solid hardwood, see our detailed comparison: Engineered vs Solid Hardwood Flooring.

Popular Hardwood Species in Round Rock

Our Round Rock customers tend to gravitate toward species that balance durability with aesthetics. The top choices include:

  • White Oak — The most popular species in Round Rock right now. Its tight grain pattern resists water penetration better than red oak, and it takes stain beautifully. Rated 1,360 on the Janka hardness scale, white oak handles the daily demands of active family households in Teravista and Forest Creek.
  • Hickory — With a Janka rating of 1,820, hickory is one of the hardest domestic species available. Its dramatic grain variation gives floors a rustic, character-rich appearance that pairs well with the Texas hill country aesthetic. An excellent choice for homes with dogs or high foot traffic.
  • Acacia — Known for its striking color variation and exotic grain patterns, acacia has become popular in Round Rock's newer homes where homeowners want a distinctive look. It is extremely hard (Janka 2,300+) and handles wear exceptionally well.
  • Red Oak — A classic choice with warm, pinkish undertones. Red oak is slightly softer than white oak but remains a durable and cost-effective option for bedrooms, dining rooms, and formal living areas.

What to Expect During Installation

Our hardwood installation process for Round Rock homes follows a proven sequence:

  1. Free in-home consultation. We visit your Round Rock home, measure each room, discuss your goals, and provide a detailed written estimate. We bring samples so you can see how different species and stains look in your actual lighting conditions.
  2. Material delivery and acclimation. Hardwood needs to adjust to your home's temperature and humidity before installation. We deliver material 5 to 7 days early and store it in the rooms where it will be installed.
  3. Subfloor preparation. We remove existing flooring, test the concrete slab for moisture levels, repair any issues, and ensure the surface is flat and clean. This step is critical for long-term performance.
  4. Installation. Depending on the product and slab conditions, we use glue-down or floating installation methods. Our installers maintain tight seams, consistent plank staggering, and proper expansion gaps throughout.
  5. Finishing. We install transitions, baseboards, and quarter round. A thorough cleanup and walkthrough with you ensures everything meets your expectations.

Hardwood Flooring Costs in Round Rock

Hardwood flooring costs in Round Rock are in line with the broader Austin metro market:

  • Engineered hardwood (material + labor): $7 to $13 per square foot
  • Solid hardwood (material + labor): $8 to $15 per square foot
  • Old flooring removal: $1 to $3 per square foot
  • Subfloor prep and leveling: $1 to $3 per square foot if needed

For a typical 1,500-square-foot main level installation, Round Rock homeowners can expect to invest between $10,500 and $19,500 depending on species, plank width, and project complexity.
