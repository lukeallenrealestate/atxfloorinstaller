Hardwood Floor Installation in Westlake Hills, TX

Westlake Hills is one of Austin's most prestigious communities, and its homeowners expect nothing less than exceptional quality in every detail of their homes. ATX Floor Installer provides premium hardwood floor installation for Westlake Hills residences, from the sprawling estates along Westlake Drive and in Rob Roy to the elegant homes of Davenport Ranch, Lost Creek, and the Eanes ISD corridor. Our work in Westlake Hills reflects the same standard of craftsmanship that defines this extraordinary neighborhood.

Luxury Hardwood for Westlake Hills Homes

Westlake Hills homes are not cookie-cutter builds. They are architect-designed residences with custom millwork, floor-to-ceiling windows capturing hill country views, and interiors that demand flooring materials of the highest caliber. The hardwood we install in Westlake Hills reflects that standard:

  • Wide-Plank European Oak — The signature choice for Westlake Hills. Available in planks up to 10 inches wide with wire-brushed, fumed, or hand-scraped textures that add depth and character. European oak features a tighter, more refined grain than American white oak and is the standard in luxury residential design.
  • French Oak — Sourced from managed forests in France, French oak offers exceptional color consistency, subtle grain patterns, and a warm undertone that complements the natural stone and custom cabinetry common in Westlake Hills kitchens and great rooms.
  • American White Oak — A classic that never goes out of style. For Westlake Hills homeowners who prefer a traditional look, white oak in a natural or light stain creates a timeless foundation. Janka hardness of 1,360 provides excellent durability.
  • Walnut — Rich, dark chocolate tones that make a dramatic statement in formal dining rooms, studies, and master suites. Walnut is softer than oak (Janka 1,010) and is best suited for rooms with moderate traffic.

Custom Stain Matching and Design Coordination

Many Westlake Hills projects involve working with interior designers and architects to achieve a specific vision. We regularly coordinate with design teams to custom-match stain colors, select plank widths that complement room proportions, and create transitions between different flooring materials that are seamless and intentional. Whether you need a weathered grey European oak to anchor a contemporary open-concept space or a rich espresso walnut for a traditional library, we achieve exact color matches through custom stain blending and sample approval before any floor goes down.

Hill Country Challenges for Hardwood

Westlake Hills homes present specific challenges that require experienced installation:

  • Intense UV exposure. Hillside homes with expansive west-facing windows receive tremendous sunlight. UV exposure can darken certain wood species and lighten stains over time. We recommend UV-resistant finishes and discuss window treatment options to protect your investment.
  • Multi-level installations. Many Westlake Hills homes span multiple levels built into the hillside. Hardwood on stairs, landings, and transitional areas between levels requires precise custom work that our team specializes in.
  • Radiant heat systems. Several Westlake Hills homes feature in-floor radiant heating in bathrooms and master suites. Engineered hardwood with specific thickness and adhesive requirements is essential over radiant systems. See our guide on flooring over radiant heat.
  • Concrete slab and pier-and-beam foundations. Unlike many Austin suburbs that are exclusively slab-on-grade, Westlake Hills has both foundation types. We select the appropriate installation method for each: glue-down or floating for slabs, nail-down for pier-and-beam with wood subfloors.

Refinishing Existing Hardwood in Westlake Hills

Many established Westlake Hills homes have existing hardwood floors that have dulled, scratched, or fallen out of step with updated interiors. Before replacing, consider hardwood refinishing. Sanding down to bare wood and applying a contemporary stain can transform golden oak into the modern grey or natural tones that Westlake Hills homeowners prefer today. Refinishing costs a fraction of replacement and preserves the original character of the wood.
