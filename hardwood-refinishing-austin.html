Hardwood Floor Refinishing in Austin, TX

Your hardwood floors have good bones. Beneath the scratches, dull finish, and years of wear, the natural beauty of the wood is still there, waiting to be uncovered. Hardwood floor refinishing is one of the most transformative home improvements you can make, and at ATX Floor Installer, we have been restoring hardwood floors across Greater Austin for over 15 years.

Refinishing costs a fraction of replacement and can make your floors look brand new in just a few days. Whether your oak floors have pet scratches, your walnut stain has faded, or you simply want to change the color of your hardwood, our team delivers results that exceed expectations every time.

When to Refinish vs. When to Replace

Not every worn hardwood floor needs to be torn out. In most cases, refinishing is the better option. Here is how to decide:

Refinishing Is the Right Choice When:

  • Surface scratches, scuffs, and wear marks are visible but the wood underneath is solid
  • The existing finish has dulled, yellowed, or worn through in high-traffic areas
  • You want to change the stain color or go from a glossy to matte finish
  • There is enough wood thickness remaining to sand (solid hardwood can typically be refinished 3 to 5 times)
  • Water damage is limited to the surface and has not caused structural warping

Replacement Is Necessary When:

  • Boards are warped, buckled, or structurally damaged
  • Extensive water damage has reached the subfloor
  • Previous refinishing has sanded the boards too thin (less than 3/4 inch remaining on solid hardwood)
  • You want to change to a different species or plank width
  • Termite or pest damage has compromised the wood

During your free in-home inspection, we assess the condition of your floors and give you an honest recommendation. If refinishing will get you the results you want, that is what we recommend. If replacement makes more sense, we will tell you that too.

Our Refinishing Process

Every hardwood refinishing project follows a careful, multi-step process. Rushing any step compromises the final result, which is why we take the time to do each one right.

  1. Inspection and preparation. We examine every board, identify any repairs needed (loose boards, gaps, damaged sections), and address them before sanding begins. We move furniture, tape off adjacent rooms, and protect surfaces that are not being refinished.
  2. Coarse sanding. Using professional drum and edge sanders, we remove the old finish and a thin layer of wood to expose a clean, fresh surface. This is where scratches, stains, and surface damage disappear.
  3. Medium and fine sanding. We progress through increasingly fine grits to smooth the wood and prepare it for stain. This multi-pass approach is what creates a truly smooth, professional finish.
  4. Stain application (optional). If you are changing the color of your floors, we apply your chosen stain evenly and consistently. We recommend testing stain on a small area first so you can see exactly how it will look on your specific wood.
  5. Sealing and finishing coats. We apply two to three coats of polyurethane or your chosen finish, with light sanding between coats for maximum adhesion. Each coat needs proper drying time, which we carefully manage based on Austin's temperature and humidity conditions.
  6. Final inspection and cleanup. We do a thorough walkthrough with you, clean up all dust and debris, and make sure you are completely happy with the results.

Dustless Sanding Technology

Traditional floor sanding produces enormous amounts of fine dust that settles on every surface in your home. Our dustless sanding equipment uses high-powered vacuum systems attached directly to the sanders, capturing up to 99% of dust at the source.

This means cleaner air during the project, less cleanup afterward, and a healthier environment for your family. Dustless sanding is especially important for homes with allergy sufferers, asthma, or young children. It also produces a better finish because there is less dust contaminating the wet polyurethane between coats.

Popular Stain Colors in Austin

Stain color is a personal choice, and trends in Austin have shifted over the years. Here are the most popular options we see right now:

  • Natural (clear coat only) — Lets the wood's natural color and grain show through. The most popular choice for white oak floors.
  • Jacobean — A rich, dark brown that adds depth and warmth. Beautiful on oak and looks stunning in traditional and transitional homes.
  • Dark Walnut — Slightly lighter than Jacobean with warm brown undertones. A versatile choice that works with almost any decor style.
  • Provincial — A medium brown that enhances grain without dramatically changing the wood's natural color. Popular in Westlake Hills and Lakeway homes.
  • Special Walnut — A balanced medium-dark tone that is neither too warm nor too cool. One of the most requested colors in Austin.
  • Weathered Oak — A light, gray-toned finish that creates a modern, coastal aesthetic. Increasingly popular in new Austin construction.
  • True Black — A bold, dramatic choice for contemporary spaces. Creates a striking contrast with white walls and light furnishings.

We bring stain samples to your home so you can see them on your actual wood, in your actual lighting. What looks perfect in a showroom can look completely different in your living room.

Drying Time and What to Expect

Refinishing is not an overnight project. Here is a realistic timeline for a typical Austin refinishing job:

  • Sanding: 1 to 2 days depending on square footage and condition
  • Staining: 1 day, with 24 hours drying time before the first coat of finish
  • Finish coats: 1 coat per day, with overnight drying between coats (2 to 3 coats total)
  • Light foot traffic: 24 to 48 hours after the final coat (socks only, no shoes)
  • Furniture replacement: 3 to 5 days after the final coat
  • Full cure: 2 to 4 weeks (avoid area rugs and heavy furniture during this period)

Austin's humidity can affect drying times, especially during summer. We monitor conditions closely and adjust our schedule to ensure each coat cures properly before applying the next.

Hardwood Refinishing Costs in Austin

Refinishing is one of the best values in home improvement. Compared to new hardwood installation at $8 to $15 per square foot, refinishing delivers a like-new floor at a fraction of the cost:

  • Sand and refinish (no stain change): $3 to $5 per square foot
  • Sand, stain, and refinish: $4 to $7 per square foot
  • Board repairs (if needed): $5 to $15 per board
  • Typical 1,000 sq ft project: $3,000 to $7,000
