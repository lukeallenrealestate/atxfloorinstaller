LVP Flooring Installation in Austin, TX

Luxury vinyl plank has become one of the most popular flooring choices in Austin, and for good reason. It delivers the look of real hardwood with waterproof performance, outstanding durability, and a price point that fits more budgets. At ATX Floor Installer, we have installed LVP in hundreds of Austin homes and know exactly how to get the best results from this versatile material.

Whether you are renovating a kitchen, upgrading your entire first floor, or finishing a bathroom, LVP handles it all. And in a city where temperatures and humidity can shift rapidly, luxury vinyl plank holds up where other flooring materials struggle.

Why LVP Is Ideal for Austin Homes

Austin's climate presents unique challenges for flooring. Summer humidity can climb above 80%, while winter brings drier air that causes natural wood to contract. LVP is engineered to remain stable through these fluctuations, which makes it one of the most practical choices for Central Texas homeowners.

  • 100% waterproof core — No warping, swelling, or damage from spills, pet accidents, or moisture. Install it in kitchens, bathrooms, laundry rooms, and entryways with confidence.
  • Humidity stable — Unlike solid hardwood, LVP does not expand and contract with Austin's seasonal humidity swings.
  • Scratch-resistant wear layer — Pet claws, kids' toys, and heavy furniture are no match for a quality LVP wear layer rated at 20 mil or higher.
  • Easy maintenance — Sweep and damp-mop. No waxing, no refinishing, no special cleaners required.

Click-Lock vs. Glue-Down LVP

There are two primary installation methods for luxury vinyl plank, and we are experienced with both. The right choice depends on your subfloor, the room, and how you plan to use the space.

Click-Lock (Floating Installation)

Click-lock planks snap together along tongue-and-groove edges and float over the subfloor without adhesive. This is the most common method for residential installations. It is faster, creates less mess, and allows the floor to be removed or replaced without damaging the subfloor. Click-lock LVP works well over concrete, plywood, and even existing tile or vinyl.

Glue-Down

Glue-down LVP is adhered directly to the subfloor with a specialized adhesive. This method creates an extremely solid, quiet floor with zero movement underfoot. It is the preferred method for commercial spaces, large open areas, and rooms with heavy rolling traffic like offices or kitchens with island seating. Glue-down installation requires a perfectly flat, clean subfloor.

Perfect for Families and Pet Owners

If you have dogs, cats, or young children, LVP is one of the smartest flooring investments you can make. The waterproof core means accidents are never an emergency. The scratch-resistant wear layer stands up to claws and toys. And unlike carpet, LVP does not trap allergens, pet dander, or odors.

We regularly install LVP for Austin families who want a floor that looks like hardwood but can handle the reality of daily life with kids and pets. Many of our customers tell us they wish they had made the switch sooner.

LVP Flooring Costs in Austin

One of the biggest advantages of LVP is its value. You get the look of hardwood or stone at a fraction of the cost, and the installation is typically faster. Here are typical price ranges for our Austin projects:

  • Entry-level LVP (material + labor): $4 to $7 per square foot
  • Mid-range LVP (material + labor): $7 to $10 per square foot
  • Premium LVP (material + labor): $10 to $14 per square foot
  • Subfloor preparation (if needed): $1 to $3 per square foot

Compared to hardwood at $8 to $15 per square foot, LVP delivers significant savings without sacrificing appearance. Schedule a free in-home estimate for exact pricing on your project.

Our LVP Installation Process

  1. Free in-home consultation — We measure your space, discuss your needs, and show you product samples.
  2. Subfloor inspection and prep — We check for moisture, flatness, and structural issues. Any problems are corrected before installation.
  3. Old flooring removal — We remove and dispose of existing carpet, tile, or vinyl as needed.
  4. LVP installation — Planks are laid with precision, maintaining proper expansion gaps and staggering joints for a natural look.
  5. Trim, transitions, and cleanup — We install all trim pieces, clean up thoroughly, and walk you through the finished floor.

Most LVP installations in Austin are completed in one to two days, depending on the size of the area. Your home is back to normal fast.
