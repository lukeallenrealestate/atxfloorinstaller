Tile Installation in Austin, TX

Tile is one of the most versatile and long-lasting flooring materials available, and it is a natural fit for Austin homes. From sleek porcelain in a modern kitchen to natural stone in a spa-like bathroom, tile delivers beauty and performance that lasts for decades. ATX Floor Installer provides expert tile installation for floors, walls, backsplashes, showers, and entryways throughout the Greater Austin area.

Our tile installers have over 15 years of experience working with every type of tile material and layout pattern. We take pride in precise cuts, level surfaces, clean grout lines, and finished results that look professional from every angle.

Porcelain vs. Ceramic vs. Natural Stone

The type of tile you choose affects the look, durability, maintenance, and cost of your project. Here is how the three main categories compare.

Porcelain Tile

Porcelain is fired at higher temperatures than ceramic, making it denser, harder, and more water-resistant. It is rated for both indoor and outdoor use, handles heavy foot traffic, and comes in an extraordinary range of styles, including options that convincingly mimic hardwood, marble, and concrete. Porcelain is our most recommended tile for Austin kitchens, bathrooms, and entryways.

Ceramic Tile

Ceramic tile is made from the same base materials as porcelain but is less dense and more porous. It is easier to cut and slightly less expensive, making it a popular choice for wall applications, backsplashes, and lighter-traffic floors. Ceramic is available in a wide variety of colors, shapes, and patterns.

Natural Stone

Travertine, marble, slate, and limestone bring a one-of-a-kind organic beauty to any space. Each piece is unique, and natural stone creates a high-end look that is difficult to replicate. Stone tile requires sealing to protect against staining and moisture absorption, and we include initial sealing as part of every natural stone installation.

Where We Install Tile

Tile works in almost every room of your home. These are the most common applications we handle for Austin homeowners:

  • Kitchen floors — Porcelain and ceramic tile stand up to spills, dropped pans, and constant foot traffic. Large-format tiles with minimal grout lines create a clean, modern look.
  • Bathrooms — From floor-to-ceiling shower tile to heated bathroom floors, we handle full bathroom tile installations including waterproofing, shower pans, and niche construction.
  • Entryways and mudrooms — Your entryway takes more abuse than almost any other floor in your home. Tile handles wet shoes, dirt, and heavy use without showing wear.
  • Backsplashes — Subway tile, mosaic, herringbone, or statement patterns. A well-designed backsplash ties your kitchen or bathroom together and protects walls from moisture.
  • Laundry rooms — Waterproof and easy to clean, tile is the practical choice for laundry areas where leaks and spills are a real possibility.
  • Outdoor patios and pool decks — With the right porcelain or stone product, we install tile for covered patios and outdoor living spaces.

Popular Tile Patterns and Layouts

The pattern you choose changes the entire feel of a room. Our installers are skilled in all of these popular layouts:

  • Straight lay (grid) — Clean, classic, and timeless. Tiles are set in a simple grid pattern with aligned grout lines. Best for modern and minimalist spaces.
  • Herringbone — Rectangular tiles are laid at 45-degree angles in a zigzag pattern. Adds visual movement and a high-end feel to floors and backsplashes.
  • Subway — A classic offset pattern originally used in New York subway stations. Perfect for backsplashes and shower walls. Available in traditional 3x6 and modern oversized formats.
  • Chevron — Similar to herringbone but with angled edges that create a continuous V-shape. More contemporary and visually dynamic.
  • Diagonal — Standard square tiles rotated 45 degrees. This layout makes rooms feel larger and adds visual interest to simple tile selections.
  • Basketweave and mosaic — Intricate patterns that work well in smaller spaces like bathroom floors, shower floors, and decorative accents.

Grout and Waterproofing

Grout and waterproofing are where many tile installations fail. We take these critical steps seriously.

We use high-quality sanded and unsanded grout matched to your tile color and application. For wet areas like showers, tub surrounds, and bathroom floors, we install a complete waterproofing system beneath the tile, including membrane barriers and properly sloped shower pans. This prevents water from reaching your subfloor and causing mold, rot, or structural damage.

We also offer epoxy grout for areas that need maximum moisture resistance and stain protection, such as kitchen countertops and shower floors.

Tile Installation Costs in Austin

Tile costs depend on the material, tile size, pattern complexity, and whether waterproofing or substrate work is needed. Here are typical ranges for our Austin projects:

  • Ceramic tile (material + labor): $6 to $12 per square foot
  • Porcelain tile (material + labor): $8 to $16 per square foot
  • Natural stone (material + labor): $12 to $25 per square foot
  • Backsplash installation: $15 to $30 per square foot (smaller area, more detail work)
  • Shower tile (full waterproofing included): $20 to $35 per square foot
