Tile Installation in Cedar Park, TX

Whether you are remodeling a master bathroom in Buttercup Creek, upgrading the kitchen floor in Twin Creeks, or replacing dated entryway tile in Anderson Mill, ATX Floor Installer delivers professional tile work for Cedar Park homeowners. Tile is one of the most durable and versatile flooring materials available, and in the wet areas of your home it remains the gold standard. Our experienced tile installers handle everything from simple floor replacements to complex shower builds, backsplash installations, and whole-room remodels.

Upgrading Dated Tile in Cedar Park's Established Neighborhoods

Cedar Park homes built in the 1990s and 2000s around Buttercup Creek, Anderson Mill, and Cypress Canyon typically feature small-format ceramic tile (12-by-12 or 13-by-13 inches) in bathrooms, kitchens, and entryways. This tile was installed as a basic, functional material and was never meant to be a design statement. After two decades of use, it shows its age: chipped corners, cracked tiles from settling foundations, stained grout that no amount of cleaning can restore, and color schemes that feel locked in the past.

Replacing this old tile with modern large-format porcelain is one of the highest-impact upgrades a Cedar Park homeowner can make. A bathroom that goes from 12-by-12 beige ceramic to 12-by-24 grey porcelain with matching wall tile looks like a completely different room. The fewer grout lines create a cleaner, more contemporary appearance, and the improved porcelain body resists stains and moisture far better than the old ceramic.

Popular Tile Projects in Cedar Park

Master Bathroom Remodels

The most requested tile project in Cedar Park is the master bathroom renovation. This typically involves removing old tub-shower combos and replacing them with walk-in showers featuring floor-to-ceiling porcelain tile, linear drains, and frameless glass enclosures. We handle the complete installation including waterproofing membrane, proper slope to drain, wall tile, floor tile, and niches for soap and shampoo. Cedar Park homeowners in Twin Creeks and Ranch at Brushy Creek are increasingly choosing large-format tile in the shower (fewer grout lines to clean) with accent mosaic tile as a feature strip or niche lining. Learn more on our bathroom flooring page.

Kitchen Floor and Backsplash

Kitchen tile in Cedar Park runs the gamut from replacing old ceramic floors with wood-look porcelain planks to installing designer backsplashes behind new countertops. Porcelain tile remains the smartest kitchen floor choice because it is completely waterproof, will not stain from cooking spills, and handles dropped pots and pans better than any other material. For backsplashes, subway tile remains popular but Cedar Park homeowners are increasingly choosing stacked vertical layouts, zellige-style handmade tile, and bold geometric patterns. See our kitchen flooring guide.

Entryway and Mudroom Tile

Cedar Park's position along the 183A corridor means rainstorms can arrive quickly, and a tiled entryway or mudroom provides the durability and easy cleanup that families need. Textured porcelain in dark tones hides dirt and provides slip resistance, making it both practical and attractive as the first surface visitors see.

Heated Tile Floors

Electric radiant heating mats installed beneath tile create a luxurious warm floor in master bathrooms and is one of the most popular upgrades we install in Cedar Park homes. The system adds $4 to $8 per square foot to the tile installation cost but provides comfort that homeowners love, especially during Austin's brief but chilly winter months. For details, see our guide on flooring over radiant heat.
