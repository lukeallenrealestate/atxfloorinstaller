Tile Installation in Georgetown, TX

Georgetown's diverse housing market creates a wide range of tile installation opportunities, from accessible bathroom upgrades in Sun City to luxury shower builds in Cimarron Hills and kitchen remodels in Wolf Ranch. ATX Floor Installer provides professional tile work throughout Georgetown, handling everything from replacing dated builder-grade ceramic to crafting custom natural stone installations that elevate the most discerning homes.

Sun City Bathroom Tile Upgrades

Bathroom tile upgrades are the number one tile project we perform in Sun City. After 15 to 20 years, the original builder tile in Sun City bathrooms shows its age: grout has stained and cracked, tiles have chipped, and the small-format designs look dated. More importantly, many Sun City residents are upgrading their bathrooms for accessibility and safety.

Our most popular Sun City bathroom tile projects include:

  • Walk-in shower conversions. Replacing tub-shower combos with curbless or low-curb walk-in showers featuring non-slip porcelain floor tile, full-height wall tile, and built-in seating. These showers are easier and safer to use while looking modern and elegant.
  • Non-slip floor tile. Replacing smooth ceramic with textured porcelain that provides better traction when wet. We carefully select tile with appropriate Coefficient of Friction (COF) ratings for wet bathroom floors.
  • Comfort-height upgrades. When retiling, many Sun City homeowners also upgrade to comfort-height vanities and grab bars, creating a complete bathroom refresh that improves daily life. Visit our bathroom flooring page for more options.

Kitchen Tile in Georgetown Homes

Georgetown kitchens are getting bigger and more open in newer communities like Wolf Ranch and Georgetown Village. Tile plays multiple roles in these spaces:

  • Floor tile. Large-format porcelain (12-by-24 or 24-by-24) in wood-look or stone-look patterns creates a clean, modern kitchen floor that handles spills, foot traffic, and dropped cookware. Many Georgetown homeowners extend the same tile into the dining area and breakfast nook for visual continuity.
  • Backsplash tile. The kitchen backsplash has become a design statement in Georgetown homes. Subway tile remains a reliable classic, but we are installing more and more patterned tile, vertical stacks, herringbone layouts, and handmade zellige tile that adds texture and personality.
  • Island accent tile. A growing trend in Georgetown: adding decorative tile to the front face of kitchen islands. This detail adds visual interest and ties the island to the backsplash design. See our kitchen flooring guide.

Outdoor and Patio Tile

Georgetown homeowners with covered patios and outdoor living spaces are increasingly adding porcelain tile to these areas. Outdoor-rated porcelain with textured surfaces provides a beautiful, durable floor that handles sun, rain, and temperature extremes. For homes in Berry Creek and Cimarron Hills with expansive outdoor entertaining spaces, patio tile creates a seamless indoor-outdoor flow that adds both function and value.

Historic Downtown Georgetown Tile

Georgetown's historic square and surrounding downtown neighborhoods contain homes built from the 1880s through the 1940s. These properties often feature original tile work in bathrooms and entryways that is worth preserving or restoring. We work carefully with period-appropriate tile, including hex mosaics, subway tile, and encaustic patterns that maintain the historic character while providing a refreshed, clean appearance. Where original tile cannot be saved, we source reproduction tile that matches the home's era and style.

Tile Costs in Georgetown
