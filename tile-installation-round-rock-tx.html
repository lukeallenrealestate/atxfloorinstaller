Tile Installation in Round Rock, TX

Tile remains one of the most versatile and durable flooring options for Round Rock homeowners, especially in kitchens, bathrooms, entryways, and laundry rooms where water resistance and easy cleaning are priorities. ATX Floor Installer provides professional tile installation throughout Round Rock, from master bathroom remodels in Teravista to kitchen floor upgrades in Forest Creek and full-home tile installations in newer communities like Paloma Lake and Walsh Ranch.

Replacing Builder-Grade Tile in Round Rock Homes

A significant portion of Round Rock's housing stock was built between the mid-1990s and today, and many of these homes were delivered with basic 12-by-12 or 13-by-13 ceramic tile in bathrooms, kitchens, and entryways. This builder-grade tile was selected for the developer's budget, not for aesthetics or longevity. After a decade or two of use, it shows its age: chipped edges, cracked tiles, stained grout, and a dated look that drags down the rest of the home's appeal.

Upgrading to modern porcelain tile transforms these spaces. Large-format tiles (18-by-18, 24-by-24, or even 12-by-24 planks) create a clean, contemporary look with fewer grout lines and a more seamless appearance. The difference between a bathroom with old 12-by-12 beige ceramic and one with large-format grey porcelain is night and day.

Popular Tile Options for Round Rock Homes

Porcelain Tile

Porcelain is our top recommendation for Round Rock homes. It is denser and harder than standard ceramic, with a water absorption rate below 0.5 percent. This makes it virtually impervious to moisture, stains, and wear. Porcelain tile comes in an enormous range of designs, including wood-look planks, natural stone looks, concrete-look, and geometric patterns. For Round Rock kitchens and bathrooms, porcelain offers the best combination of durability and design flexibility.

Wood-Look Porcelain Tile

Wood-look porcelain tile has become extremely popular in Round Rock, particularly for homeowners who want the warmth of a wood aesthetic with zero moisture concerns. These tiles come in plank shapes (typically 6-by-36 or 8-by-48 inches) with realistic grain textures and color variations. They are perfect for open-concept living areas where you want a continuous wood look that extends seamlessly into kitchens and bathrooms. For a comparison with real hardwood, see our guide on tile vs hardwood for kitchens.

Natural Stone

Marble, travertine, and slate bring a premium, organic feel to Round Rock homes. Natural stone is particularly popular for master bathroom floors, shower walls, and kitchen backsplashes in the more upscale Round Rock neighborhoods like Behrens Ranch and Walsh Ranch. Stone requires sealing and slightly more maintenance than porcelain, but the aesthetic is unmatched.

Tile Installation by Room

Kitchen Tile

Round Rock kitchens see heavy traffic, food spills, and water exposure around sinks and dishwashers. Porcelain tile with a textured surface provides slip resistance and easy cleanup. Many homeowners extend their kitchen tile into the adjacent dining area for a seamless flow. We also install kitchen backsplashes in subway, mosaic, and large-format designs. See our kitchen flooring guide for more options.

Bathroom Tile

Bathrooms are where tile truly shines. We handle everything from simple floor replacements to complete bathroom remodels including shower tile, tub surrounds, and heated tile floors. Walk-in showers with frameless glass and floor-to-ceiling tile have become the most requested bathroom upgrade in Round Rock. Our installers ensure proper waterproofing membrane installation, correct slope to drains, and consistent grout lines. Visit our bathroom flooring page for detailed options.

Entryway and Mudroom Tile

The entryway sets the tone for your entire home. In Round Rock, where rainstorms can send muddy shoes through the front door, a durable porcelain tile entryway provides both beauty and practicality. Darker colors and textured surfaces hide dirt between cleanings, and tile stands up to heavy foot traffic without showing wear patterns.
